# A08 · The Full Process of Scale Development: From Construct Definition to Formal Administration

> A deeper version accompanying Q05. It can be split into Xiaohongshu cards for the "Eight Steps of Scale Development" series.

## Scale Development is an Engineering Task, Not a Writing Task

"Creating a questionnaire" sounds like a writing task, but in reality, it is a measurement engineering task: needs analysis (construct definition), prototype (item pool), review (content validity), testing (pilot), iteration (psychometric item screening), acceptance (CFA/reliability), and documentation (user manual). This article walks through the entire process step by step, providing deliverables and common pitfalls at each stage.

## Step 1: Construct Definition — 90% of Failures Are Determined Here

**Deliverable**: One page of a **construct specification document**, including: theoretical definition (a paragraph), dimension division and rationale, examples of behavioral manifestations for each dimension, and a **boundary statement** (what this construct does not include, and how it differentiates from neighboring constructs).

**Common Pitfall**: Skipping the definition and directly writing items. The consequences will become apparent in two months — the dimensions derived from factor analysis will not match your expectations, because you never clearly envisioned them. **Judgment criterion**: Can you explain in two minutes to an outsider what you are measuring and what you are not?

## Step 2: Item Pool — Produce According to the Blueprint, with Excess Reserve

Distribute the number of items according to dimensions, and reserve 2–3 times the number of items expected (half of the items will not make it to the final version). Sources of items: borrowing from mature scales (note authorization and translation standards), extracting original quotes from target population interviews (the best items often come from this), and expert experience.

**Item Writing Discipline**: One item per meaning ("content that is both interesting and useful" is two items); avoid negative phrasing, especially double negatives; match the language to the reading level of the target population (a scale for elementary students should read like what an elementary student would say); be cautious with reverse-scored items (the cost of method effects often outweighs the benefit of preventing careless responding, see my article on reverse-scored items).

## Step 3: Content Review and Cognitive Interviewing — Two Quality Checks Before Data Collection

**Expert Review**: 5–8 domain experts rate each item (relevance, clarity, representativeness), calculate CVI, and decide to keep, revise, or delete items. Simultaneously, ask experts to review fairness: does the item context favor specific regional, gender, or class experiences?

**Cognitive Interviewing**: 8–15 target participants complete the questionnaire aloud, thinking out loud: "How did you understand this question? Why did you choose this option?" The concept of "using fragmented time for learning" you envisioned may be interpreted by participants as "slacking off at work" — such understanding biases can only be captured through interviews, not through statistics.

## Step 4: Pilot Testing — First Encounter with Real Data

Sample size: 100–300. Look at four things: response distribution (if 90% of respondents choose the same option for a question = no discrimination information, candidate for deletion); total item correlation (<0.3 is a warning); preliminary dimension α; open feedback (which items are hard to understand). This round eliminates clearly unsuitable items, and the number of items converges toward the final version.

## Step 5: EFA — Let the Structure Emerge Naturally

Conduct exploratory factor analysis (EFA) on a new sample (or the pilot sample, if the pilot sample is sufficient):

- Use parallel analysis to determine the number of factors (`psych::fa.parallel`), do not rely solely on eigenvalues > 1;
- Use principal axis/ML extraction + oblique rotation (dimensions of constructs are almost always correlated);
- Deletion rules: factor loadings < 0.40, cross-factor double loadings (difference < 0.20), low communality;
- **Delete one item at a time and rerun the analysis** — the factor structure will change with item deletion;
- If the emerging structure does not match the theoretical blueprint, go back to the construct specification document and clarify — data-driven "renaming dimensions" requires theoretical awareness, not just giving factors arbitrary names.

## Step 6: CFA + Reliability — Acceptance on an Independent Sample

**Must use an independent sample** (using the same data for EFA and CFA is circular reasoning, which is a common reason for rejection in scale papers). Test the structure determined by EFA: fit indices (CFI/TLI, RMSEA, SRMR), standardized loadings, and correlations between dimensions (high correlations suggest redundant dimensions). Also report ω and α for each dimension.

**Advanced Bonus Points**: Measurement invariance (configural → metric → scalar across gender/year level), only with this can you compare group means; IRT item analysis (use GRM/GPCM for polytomous scoring), use item information curves to check measurement precision for each dimension in the target ability range, and replace low-information items. **The dividing line between academic scales and formal assessment tools is here** — the latter must pass the IRT and DIF checks.

## Step 7: Criterion and Norms

Evidence of association with external variables (aggregation, differentiation, criterion-related validity, see the long article on validity). If the scale is to output "high/medium/low" judgments, it needs a normative sample (representative reference group distribution) or a standard-setting procedure to define boundaries — **"scoring above X indicates high anxiety" without norms is unfounded**.

## Step 8: User Manual — From "A Set of Items" to "A Tool"

Content list: construct definition, target population and scenarios, administration instructions, scoring rules (including reverse-scored items and missing data handling), summary of validity and reliability evidence, norm tables/boundary criteria, and citation format. The manual is a prerequisite for others to correctly use and cite the scale, and it is also the complete form of the scale as an academic product.

## Honest Budget for Time and Sample

The full process requires 2–4 independent samples (pilot, EFA, CFA, and criterion/norms can be partially merged), with a timeline of six months to two years. Realistic simplification for a thesis: merge pilot and EFA, use split-half samples for CFA — the bottom line is that **EFA and CFA must come from different sources**. Further simplification is betting that reviewers don't know the field.

Scale development is the "general surgery" of psychological and educational measurement — each step has its own specialized literature. This series will expand on key processes later (IRT item screening, measurement invariance, standard setting), and we welcome your attention.
